There is a particular kind of elegance in a page that does not hurry to fill itself. It waits. It gives the eye somewhere to rest, and in doing so, it gives the mind permission to slow down.

In design, space is often mistaken for absence. But space is not empty. It is structure. It is rhythm. It is the pause between two notes that allows the melody to become clear.

## Restraint as a form of clarity

A restrained page asks fewer things from the reader. It does not compete for attention with unnecessary decoration, crowded menus, or loud gestures.

## The page as a room

A well-composed page is much like a well-composed room. There is a place to enter, a place to sit, and a clear sense of what deserves attention.

## What remains

Minimalism is not the removal of personality. It is the removal of distraction. What remains should feel human, precise, and quietly alive.
